How much prize money did PSL 2020 winners get as compared to IPL 2020?

Pakistan Super League (PSL) 2020 winners Karachi Kings won a cash prize of $500,000 (around ₹3.75 crore), while Indian Premier League (IPL) 2020 champions Mumbai Indians had won ₹20 crore. Further, PSL 2020 runners-up Lahore Qalandars got a cash prize of $200,000 (around ₹1.5 crore), while Delhi Capitals were awarded ₹12.5-crore prize money.


FIFA cancels U-17 Women's World Cup 2020, India to host in 2022

FIFA U-17 Women's World Cup, which was supposed to be held in India in 2021 after it got postponed this year due to COVID-19, was called off on Tuesday. India will now be hosting the next edition in 2022. The U-20 Women's World Cup in Costa Rica was also cancelled and they were again named hosts for the 2022 edition.


Germany record worst defeat in football since 1931, lose to Spain 0-6

Spain's Ferran Torres scored a hat-trick to lead his side to 6-0 win in the UEFA Nations League and hand Germany their worst defeat since 1931, when they also lost 0-6 to Austria in a friendly. Meanwhile, Germany's only five-goal loss in a competitive game had been West Germany's 8-3 defeat to Hungary at the 1954 World Cup group stage.

Sachin's debut, last int'l innings were on same day 24 years apart

Sachin Tendulkar made his international debut on November 15, 1989, and played his last innings in international cricket on the same date 24 years later in 2013. Sachin debuted against Pakistan in Karachi, aged 16 years 205 days, becoming the youngest Indian to play a Test. In his last innings, Sachin slammed 74(118) against West Indies at Mumbai's Wankhede Stadium.


Monday, 16-November-2020

Bradman's 100th first-class hundred came against an Indian side

Don Bradman hit his first-class career's 100th hundred, playing in his 295th innings for an Australian XI against an Indian side on November 15, 1947. Bradman hit 172 runs in the first innings, becoming the first Australian to score 100 first-class centuries. The ton was Bradman's last of his eight first-class hundreds to come in a losing cause.


Olympic medalist working as delivery boy to generate income amid COVID-19

London Olympics fencing gold medalist Rubén Limardo has been working as a delivery boy in Poland to "generate income" amid COVID-19. Limardo shared his picture on Instagram and wrote, "I can proudly say that in addition to sports I've a second job to earn a living and maintain my home." "Adjusting to change is definitely job for brave," he added.


What are new changes made to T20 format in Australia's Big Bash League?

Powerplay has been reduced to four overs in Big Bash League, while the remaining two overs can be taken by batting side as 'Power Surge' after 10 overs. An 'X-factor Player', named as 12th or 13th player, can replace a player after 10th over of first innings. 'Bash Boost' will be a bonus point awarded halfway through second innings.


Djokovic ends his sixth year as World No 1, equals Sampras' record

Serbia's Novak Djokovic finished at the top of men's tennis ranking on Sunday for the sixth time in a decade to equal the record set by former American tennis player Pete Sampras. Apart from 2020, Djokovic ended as World No 1 in 2011, 2012, 2014, 2015 and 2018. Meanwhile, Sampras topped the rankings from 1993 till 1998.

Hamilton equals Schumacher's record of winning 7 Formula One world titles

Mercedes' British driver Lewis Hamilton on Sunday won the Turkish Grand Prix to win the Formula One 2020 world championship. With this, he equalled Michael Schumacher's record of winning seven Formula One world titles. Hamilton also extended his record of winning most races in Formula One history to 94 with the win in Istanbul.
