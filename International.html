Our COVID-19 vaccine 95% effective, has no serious safety concerns: Pfizer

America's Pfizer said final results from late-stage trial of its COVID-19 vaccine, developed with Germany's BioNTech SE, show it was 95% effective with no major side effects. Pfizer has the required two-months of safety data and would apply for emergency US authorisation within days. The final analysis comes a week after initial results showed the vaccine was over 90% effective.


China has no right to pick the next Dalai Lama: US

China has no right to pick the next Dalai Lama, US Ambassador at Large for International Religious Freedom, Samuel D Brownback, said. "They have no theological basis to do that. The Tibetan Buddhists have successfully picked their leader for hundreds of years, if not longer, and they have the right to do that now," he added.


Indo-US ties will pick up, Biden no stranger to India: Jaishankar

External Affairs Minister S Jaishankar has said US President-elect Joe Biden is "not a stranger to India" as India has dealt with him in his former roles. "I am very confident that we'll pick up where we left off," he further said.

Mutations in COVID-19 appearing in Siberia: Russia

Mutations in the coronavirus are appearing in Siberia, head of Russia's consumer health watchdog, Anna Popova said on Tuesday. Popova did not give details on how contagious or deadly the mutation was considered to be but said that it would not make the virus more dangerous. This comes as Russia saw the biggest increase in daily coronavirus deaths at 442.


India, China may start producing Russia's Sputnik V vaccine: Putin

India and China may start producing Russia's Sputnik V vaccine against coronavirus, Russian President Vladimir Putin said on Tuesday. Putin also proposed that the creation of a vaccine research centre for BRICS countries be sped up. The Sputnik V vaccine has arrived in India after Dr Reddy's Laboratories got approval to conduct late-stage trials of the vaccine.


Climate change a bigger threat than COVID-19: Red Cross

Climate change poses a greater threat than coronavirus and the world should react with the same urgency to climate change as to the coronavirus crisis, the Red Cross has said. In a new report, IFRC said that it expects "climate change will have a more significant medium and long term impact on human life and on Earth."


Global coronavirus cases exceed 5.5 crore-mark

The total coronavirus cases in the world rose to 5,51,27,463 on Tuesday, according to Johns Hopkins University tracker. The US, India and Brazil accounted for around 47% of the total cases globally. The global coronavirus deaths rose to over 13.29 lakh, with the US reporting the highest fatalities at nearly 2.5 lakh followed by Brazil at over 1.66 lakh.

Scuba diver stays underwater for 145 hours in a bid to set new world record

Egyptian scuba diver Saddam Al-Kilany remained underwater for over 145 hours in a bid to set a new world record. Saddam's attempt hasn't been formally confirmed yet by Guinness World Records. In 2016, Cem Karabay set the 'longest open saltwater scuba dive (male)' Guinness World Record with a dive that lasted 142 hours, 42 minutes, 42 seconds.


World's fattest parrot 'kakapo' named New Zealand's bird of 2020, pic shared

The green and fawn kakapo, the world's heaviest and longest-living parrot, has been named as New Zealand's bird of the year 2020. This is the flightless parrot’s second win, also taking the title in 2008. Spokesperson for Bird of the Year Laura Keown said kakapo were literally brought back from the brink of extinction and their population is now 213.

New Year fireworks banned in Netherlands over COVID-19 fears

Netherlands on Friday said it's banning the sale and bursting of fireworks over New Year amid the coronavirus pandemic. The ban is to "avoid extra pressure on already overburdened care workers and to maintain public order", it added. People who violate the ban would be liable for $118 fine and a criminal record, Dutch Justice Minister Ferd Grapperhaus said.

The US on Friday recorded its biggest one-day rise in coronavirus cases as over 1.77 lakh people tested positive, The Washington Post reported. The US is the worst coronavirus-hit country in the world with nearly 1.08 crore COVID-19 cases and over 2.4 lakh deaths.
