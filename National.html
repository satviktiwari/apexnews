Sunday, 14-November-2020

Nitish elected leader of NDA in Bihar, set to become CM for 4th straight term

National Democratic Alliance (NDA) on Sunday elected Nitish Kumar as its leader at a meeting in Patna, paving way for the 69-year-old to become CM of Bihar for fourth straight term. The NDA won 125 seats in the 243-member Bihar Assembly. "I have made no claim on the CM chair, the decision will be taken by NDA," Nitish earlier said.


India records 41,100 new COVID-19 cases, total cases cross 88 lakh

With 41,100 new COVID-19 infections reported in the last 24 hours, India's total cases have surged to 88,14,579, said the Union Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) on Sunday. The death toll due to COVID-19 reached 1,29,635, with 447 new deaths reported in the last 24 hours. The number of active cases stands at 4,79,216.


32% share of stubble burning in Delhi's pollution on Diwali: Report

The share of stubble burning in Delhi's PM 2.5 pollution level accounted for 32% on Saturday, the PTI reported. The AQI in the national capital on Diwali was recorded as 414 which falls in the 'severe' category. The PM 10 level stood at 494 µg/m3 at 10 pm, close to the emergency threshold of 500 µg/m3, according to CPCB data.


Delhi CM Kejriwal, wife take part in Diwali celebrations at Akshardham Temple

Delhi Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal and his wife took part in Diwali celebrations at the Akshardham Temple today. ANI shared pictures and videos of the celebrations, including that of Kejriwal and his wife performing 'aarti' at the temple. Ministers Manish Sisodia and Satyendar Jain also took part in the celebrations.

We'll make Ayodhya glorious on world map as Vedic city: UP CM Yogi

As Ayodhya celebrated its fourth-ever Deepotsava, Uttar Pradesh CM Yogi Adityanath said, "Ayodhya will get its long lost pride. We will make it glorious on the world map as a Vedic city." "There has been a lot of injustice and humiliation for Ayodhya, which gives salvation to both birth and life. But now this will not happen," he added.


PM Modi celebrates Diwali with soldiers, takes a tank ride in Longewala; pics surface

PM Narendra Modi today celebrated Diwali with Indian soldiers at Longewala in Rajasthan's Jaisalmer. He distributed sweets to security forces personnel and was seen taking a tank ride. "No power in the world can stop our brave soldiers from guarding borders of our country. This country is happy and we are celebrating festivals because of your existence," the PM said.


India gave befitting reply to Pakistan in Battle of Longewala: PM Modi

Addressing soldiers at the Longewala post on the Rajasthan border on Saturday, Prime Minister Narendra Modi stated that Indian forces gave a befitting reply to Pakistan in Longewala in 1971. PM Modi added, "If there is one border post whose name is known by every Indian, it is Longewala... Battle of Longewala will always be remembered in history."

BJP chief to go on 100 days nationwide tour as part of 2024 elections

Friday, 13-November-2020

4 security personnel martyred, 6 civilians killed in Pak shelling along LoC

Four security personnel were martyred and six civilians were killed in multiple ceasefire violations by Pakistani troops along LoC from Gurez to Uri sectors of J&K on Friday, reported PTI. Defence spokesman Colonel Rajesh Kalia said Pakistan used mortars and other weapons and deliberately targetted civilian areas. Four other security personnel and eight other civilians were also injured.

World record set in Ayodhya with lighting of 5,84,572 diyas, pics surface

As many as 5,84,572 diyas were lit up along the banks of Saryu river in Ayodhya on the occasion of 'Deepotsav', a day before Diwali today. With this, the Guinness World Record for 'the largest display of oil lamps' was set, the Uttar Pradesh government confirmed. Last year, 4,04,026 earthen lamps were lit up in Ayodhya during 'Deepotsav' celebrations.


Finance Ministry seeks suggestions for Budget 2021-22 via email

The Finance Ministry has said it will be creating a dedicated email ID to receive suggestions from institutions and experts on the Union Budget 2021-22, instead of physical consultations, in view of COVID-19. The MyGov portal will provide a platform to receive ideas from public from November 15-30, it added. The suggestions will be examined by the concerned ministries.


WHO to set up Global Centre for Traditional Medicine in India

WHO will set up a Global Centre for Traditional Medicine in India to strengthen the research and awareness of traditional medicine, chief Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus said. "This will support WHO's efforts to implement the strategy which aims to support countries in developing policies to strengthen the role of traditional medicine as part of their journey to...a healthier world," Tedros added.
